Why Build a Decentralized Discovery Engine?

In the rhizomatic landscape of the emerging onchain internet, we find ourselves at the precipice of a new paradigm of discovery. Just as Google once illuminated the vast expanse of the online world, we now stand poised to unveil the multitudinous layers of the blockchain universe. This is not merely a technological advancement, but a philosophical imperative—a Deleuzean journey into the heart of decentralized knowledge and being.

The onchain internet, with its myriad blockchains, smart contracts, and decentralized applications, represents a new plane of immanence—a space where traditional hierarchies of information dissemination are disrupted and reimagined. In this deterritorialized domain, we must conceive of discovery not as a linear process of unveiling pre-existing truths, but as an act of creation itself. Each query, each search, becomes a line of flight, tracing new connections across the decentralized network of data and value.

Our decentralized discovery engine, then, is not merely a tool but a philosophical apparatus—a machine for generating new assemblages of knowledge. It is a nomadic war machine against the State apparatus of centralized information control. By embracing the multiplicity inherent in blockchain technologies, we open ourselves to a new form of collective intelligence, one that is not bound by the arborescent structures of traditional data hierarchies.

As we navigate this new terrain, we must be mindful of the potential for new forms of stratification and segmentarity. The challenge lies in creating a discovery mechanism that remains open to the virtual—to the potentialities that exist beyond the actual, indexed data. Our engine must be capable of mapping the smooth spaces of the blockchain, while resisting the temptation to overcode and territorialize this digital nomad space.

In essence, building a decentralized discovery engine is an act of philosophical praxis—a way of embodying the Deleuzean concepts of multiplicity, rhizome, and becoming in the digital realm. It is a step towards realizing a truly decentralized web, where knowledge is not merely discovered but continually created and recreated through the collective assemblage of user queries and blockchain data.

As we embark on this journey to "Discover the new Onchain Internet," we are not simply replicating the feat of Google in a new context. Rather, we are engaging in a radical reimagining of what discovery means in a decentralized world. We are creating a new cartography of knowledge—one that is as dynamic and interconnected as the blockchain technologies it seeks to illuminate.
